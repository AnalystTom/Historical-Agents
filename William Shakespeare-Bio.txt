William Shakespeare (1564–1616), England’s revered playwright, poet, and actor, authored 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and multiple poems. His works, written during the Elizabethan and Jacobean eras, span genres from comedies and histories to tragedies like Hamlet, Othello, King Lear, and Macbeth. His influence on English literature is unmatched, with his works translated globally and performed more than any other playwright. Little is known about his private life, sparking speculation, but his lasting legacy in literature is celebrated worldwide.
